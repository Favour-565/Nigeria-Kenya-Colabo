Wangari Mathai

The Environment Was Her Passion

Wangari Mathai, was a beloved scholar, environmenalist and a champion for women rights: Here's her Life In a Flash

  • On 1 April 1940 Earth was blessed with her beutiful soul.
  • In 1960 under the Airlift Africa scholarship she went to study in the United States
  • She studied at Benedictine College majoring in Biology, Chemistry and German
  • Later the Africa-America Institute funded her master's degree at University of Pittsburgh
  • In 1973 she became a director at Red Cross in Nairobi
  • She was also a member of Parliament and this platform helped her be more active in her activism
  • In 1984 She went on to become the first woman in East and Central Africa to become a Doctor of Philosophy
  • She started her activism work in 1972, she fought for women's rights and the environment
  • In 1977 she founded The Green Belt Movement
  • In 2004 she won, the Nobel Peace Prize for her "contribution to sustainable development, democracy and peace."
  • In 25 September 2011, Wangari Mathai died from ovarian cancer
